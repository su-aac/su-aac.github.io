If your patient or family member has been intubated with an endotracheal tube and placed on a ventilator, they may be fully conscious yet unable to produce speech. They desperately want to communicate but literally cannot speak. What can you do?

AAC offers many solutions to help these individuals communicate effectively. Today, we'll introduce an affordable and practical AAC tool: the **communication board**.

---

Communication boards display common patient needs, allowing patients to point to what they want to express. Medical staff or family members can then confirm the message. This helps healthcare providers and families understand the patient's needs and reduces the anxiety patients experience during hospitalization.

We hope this helps more people learn how AAC can solve communication challenges for ventilator patients!
